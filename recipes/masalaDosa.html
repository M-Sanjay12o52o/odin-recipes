Masala Dosa

Description:

Masala dosa is a popular South Indian dish that combines a crisp and golden crepe-like dosa with a flavorful potato filling. The dosa is made from a fermented batter of rice and lentils, resulting in a thin and lacy texture. The potato filling, known as masala, is made by saut√©ing boiled and mashed potatoes with onions, mustard seeds, curry leaves, and a blend of spices. The dosa is spread with a layer of chutney or podi (spiced powder), filled with the masala, and served with sambar and coconut chutney. The combination of the crispy dosa, the aromatic and spiced potato filling, and the tangy accompaniments creates a delicious and satisfying meal.

    For the dosa batter:

  • 1 cup rice (preferably parboiled or idli rice)
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1 tablespoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for soaking and grinding

    • For the potato masala filling:

  • 4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • A few curry leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

    • For serving:

  • Sambar (South Indian lentil stew)
  • Coconut chutney
  • Tomato chutney (optional)
